Why Movement Matters

Had an insight while cooking today (where all the best ideas come from, apparently). I was pickling radishes and realized: transformation takes time. You can't rush fermentation. You create the conditions and trust the process.

Same with voice. You can't force someone to "sing correctly." But you CAN create conditions where their natural voice emerges. Movement. Play. Character embodiment. These bypass the analytical brain and let the body remember what it already knows.

The pirate doesn't think about breath support. The pirate commands the ship.

The Research That Changed Everything

Discovered something fascinating: specialized neurons that respond ONLY to singing. Not speech. Not music. Just singing. And here's the kicker - in people who've experienced trauma or disconnection from their voice, these pathways are still there. Dormant. Waiting.

This is why character work and movement-based approaches work so well. We're not teaching new skills. We're reactivating preserved neural pathways through safe, playful exploration.

Science validates what the body already knows.
